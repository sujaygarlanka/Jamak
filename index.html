Purpose

Taking steps towards greater access to the internet
Low-Income Students
Through a non-profit that provides scholarship money to low-income engineering students in India, we came across a problem.
More Money?
When asked if they need additional money outside of the scholarship, most responded by saying a laptop would be far more useful to them than money.
Laptop Please
They were doing all their school work on their smartphones - zoom classes, reading textbooks, submitting homework, all things meant to be done on a laptop, not a smartphone.

3
Electronics Design - November 14th - December 4th
Based on our research, we put together a simple design illustrated above.
The data in our design flows from the trackpad and keyboard to the phone to control it via USB-B protocol and from the phone to the LCD screen via Miracast wireless protocol. Using USB-B and Miracast allows us to support multiple phone brands, microUSB, and multiple Android versions.
4
Physical Design - December 5th - 20th
We built the physical casing from scratch by joining a local Makerspace and using a combination of laser cutting, screws, glue and spray paint to put together our POC. A small selection from our many design iterations is shown below.

Software Design - December 20th - Today
We are currently building a Android simple app to configure the phone settings which setting the phone to tablet mode, connecting to the display and having the mirrored phone screen adjust to the aspect ratio of the LCD screen.
